Project Overview

This project addresses the need for sustainable transportation infrastructure by designing, modeling, and simulating a solar-powered electric vehicle (EV) charging station. The system integrates a photovoltaic (PV) array with a battery backup to ensure reliable, off-grid charging capabilities. [cite_start]The primary aim was to develop a high-fidelity Simulink model to evaluate the system's performance, optimize energy management, and demonstrate practical real-time monitoring using a Raspberry Pi 5. The findings validate the system's potential for sustainable EV charging and offer a scalable solution for integrating renewable energy into transportation. [cite: 6, 10, 43]

System Design & Components

Core Components

    [cite_start]
  • Photovoltaic Array: A 4.4 kW array designed to be the primary power source[cite: 7].
    • [cite_start]
  • Boost Converter: Utilizes a Perturb and Observe (P&O) MPPT algorithm to maximize power extraction from the PV array[cite: 7].
    • [cite_start]
  • Battery Storage: Includes a main 400V EV battery and a 400V backup lithium-ion battery to ensure continuous operation[cite: 7].
    • [cite_start]
  • Diode Switching: Employs a simple and effective diode-based mechanism for automatic power flow control between the PV array, backup battery, and EV battery[cite: 7].
    • [cite_start]
  • SOC Estimation: A coulomb counting method is implemented to accurately estimate the State of Charge (SOC) of the batteries[cite: 7, 44].
    • [cite_start]
  • Real-Time Monitoring: A Raspberry Pi 5 is integrated for real-time data logging and visualization of system parameters via TCP/IP[cite: 7, 49].

Simulation & Scenarios

[cite_start]A high-fidelity model was developed in Simulink using a fixed discrete solver with a 1µs time step to accurately capture system dynamics[cite: 8]. To rigorously test the design's robustness, five distinct environmental scenarios were simulated.

1. Sunny Day (Base)

[cite_start]

Ideal conditions (1000 W/m², 25°C) to test peak performance and efficiency[cite: 9, 295].

2. Low Irradiance

[cite_start]

Cloudy conditions (300 W/m²) to evaluate the backup battery's contribution[cite: 9, 295].

3. High Temperature

[cite_start]

Hot conditions (45°C) to assess performance under thermal stress[cite: 9, 295].

4. Freezing Conditions

[cite_start]

Cold temperatures (-5°C) without backup power to test PV-only operation[cite: 9, 295].

5. No Solar Input

[cite_start]

Tests complete reliance on the backup battery during nighttime[cite: 9, 295].

Key Findings & Results

The simulations confirmed the system's robust performance across all scenarios. [cite_start]System efficiency ranged from **75%** (backup only) to a peak of **95%** under ideal sunny conditions[cite: 9, 365].

    [cite_start]
  • The P&O MPPT algorithm effectively tracked the maximum power point, optimizing energy extraction[cite: 339, 379].
    • [cite_start]
  • The passive diode switching mechanism provided seamless transitions between the solar array and backup battery[cite: 342].
    • [cite_start]
  • The coulomb counting method for SOC estimation proved reliable, with errors between **2-5%**[cite: 9, 383].
    • [cite_start]
  • Real-time monitoring via the Raspberry Pi 5 was successfully implemented, validating the simulation results with live data logging[cite: 381].
