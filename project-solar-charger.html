Situation & Task

The rapid growth of the EV market requires sustainable charging solutions that are not grid-dependent. The primary objective of this project was to design and validate a standalone, solar-powered EV charging station capable of reliable operation under diverse environmental conditions.

My Role & Responsibilities:

As the sole engineer and researcher for this dissertation project, I was responsible for the entire project lifecycle. My tasks included conducting the literature review, defining system requirements, designing all hardware and software components, building the high-fidelity Simulink model, executing the test scenarios, analyzing the performance data, and authoring the final technical paper.

Action

I executed a multi-stage plan to bring this concept from theory to a validated design:

1. System Design & Component Sizing

I designed the architecture around a 4.4 kW PV array, a custom P&O MPPT-controlled boost converter, and a dual lithium-ion battery system. I performed all calculations to size the inductors and capacitors for the converter to ensure stable 400V output with minimal ripple.

2. Modeling & Simulation

I built a comprehensive, high-fidelity model in MATLAB/Simulink, accurately representing each component, including I-V curves, non-linear battery behavior, and high-frequency power electronics.

3. Validation & Testing

I developed a rigorous testing methodology, simulating five distinct environmental scenarios to stress-test the system's robustness, efficiency, and the reliability of the backup battery failover.

4. Real-Time Monitoring Integration

To bridge simulation with real-world application, I programmed a Raspberry Pi 5 using Python to interface with the Simulink model via TCP/IP, logging KPIs and visualizing the State of Charge (SOC) on a cloud dashboard.

Result

The project successfully validated a robust and efficient design for a standalone solar EV charging station. The key quantifiable outcomes were:

95% Peak Efficiency

Achieved under ideal sunny conditions, demonstrating minimal power loss in the conversion stage.

2-5% SOC Estimation Error

The coulomb counting algorithm proved highly accurate across all test scenarios, ensuring reliable battery management.

Seamless Power Transition

The passive diode switching mechanism reliably engaged the backup battery in low/no sun conditions without complex control logic.

Full Technical Dissertation

The project culminated in a comprehensive dissertation, detailing the design, simulation, and results, which achieved a First-Class grade.

Key Learnings

  • This project provided deep practical experience in simulating complex power electronics systems and understanding the trade-offs between component selection, efficiency, and cost.
  • I learned the critical importance of robust testing across a wide range of edge cases, as performance in extreme temperatures revealed key limitations in battery charge acceptance and sensor accuracy.
  • Integrating hardware (Raspberry Pi) with a software simulation (Simulink) taught me invaluable lessons in communication protocols (TCP/IP) and the challenges of real-time data synchronization.
